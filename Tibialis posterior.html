Tibialis posterior

The tibialis posterior is in the deep posterior compartment of the leg and is a key stabilizer of the medial longitudinal arch of the foot

Origin
Proximal posterolateral aspect of the tibia
Proximal posteromedial aspect of the fibula
Interosseous membrane

Body
Deep posterior compartment of the lower leg. Runs proximal to the medial malleolus, secured by the flexor retinaculum.

Insertion
Majorly onto the navicular
Partially onto the medial cuneiform

Innervation
Tibial Nerve, a branch of the sciatic nerve

Action
Plantarflex and invert the ankle
Stabilizing the medial longitudinal arch
